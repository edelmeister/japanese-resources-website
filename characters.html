Hiragana, Katakana & Kanji Resources

Written Japanese consists of three writing systems. Hiragana and katakana are both a phonetic alphabet. Kanji are chinese characters that represent things, ideas or concepts. You can learn more through the links below.

If you are a beginner, it's recommended to start by learning hiragana and katakana. Once you can read them both comfortably, you can move onto learning the kanji characters.

Click on any image to open the resource in a new tab.

Duolingo is an app for learning many different languages through gamification. Duolingo is great for getting started with Japanese. It won't replace courses, textboox or other resources, but is a great daily addition for your learning. You can quite easily speedrun hiragana and katakana through Duolingo. Duolingo and it's streak system have kept me motivated to study daily.

Wanikani is a website that teaches you kanji characters through mnemonics and spaced repetition. First few levels of it are free, but the whole service is paid through a monthly subscription. It will teach you to read and recognize kanji, but not write them. Through my own time with Wanikani, I'd say it's effective, but requires quite a lot of time.

Anki is a flash-card based spaced repetition system for memorizing anything you want. It's great for making your own customized learning decks for hiragana, katakana, kanji, vocabulary, and sentences. The learning curve is steep though, so I recommend checking out some tutorials first.

JPDB.io is also a flash-card based space repetition system, that is focused on kanji and vocabulary, and requires no installation or lengthy configuration. It includes a nice database of vocabulary lists from various manga, anime, games and textbooks, so you can focus on learning the kanji and vocabulary of specific material you want to be able to understand.
